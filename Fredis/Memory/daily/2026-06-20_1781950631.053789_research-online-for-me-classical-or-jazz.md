## Thu 9 Jul, 7:30pm — Sonya Yoncheva (soprano) + Malcolm Martineau (piano)

This is the jewel of your window — Yoncheva is a genuine A-list operatic soprano, and Martineau is the most sought-after song accompanist alive. But note: this is **art song (recital), not opera** — intimate, one voice and a piano, roughly 2 hrs with an interval. The confirmed running order:

- **Puccini** — *Sole e amore*, *Terra e mare*, *Menti all'avviso*, *Canto d'anime*, and after the interval *"Se come voi piccina io fossi"* from his first opera *Le Villi*. These are his rarely-heard parlour songs — the same melodic gift as *La Bohème*, shrunk to drawing-room scale.
- **Martucci** — *Al folto bosco, placida ombria* (Op. 68 No. 6). Martucci was the man who dragged Italy back toward instrumental/German seriousness in an opera-obsessed age; his songs are lush and Brahmsian.
- **Tosti** — *L'ultimo bacio*, *Ideale*. Tosti is *the* Italian salon-song composer — gorgeous, sentimental, immediately singable. *Ideale* is a recital staple.
- **Verdi** — *In solitaria stanza*, *Ad una stella*, *L'esule*. His "intimate miniatures" — early songs from the giant of Italian opera, almost never programmed.
- **Albéniz** — the Spanish strand, including his sultry *Tango*. This is where the "Italian *and* Spanish song" billing pays off.

My read: if you only do one classical night, this is it — top-tier artist, unusual repertoire you won't hear often.

## Wed 8 Jul, 7:30pm — Cantoría (Early Music)

A young Spanish vocal ensemble doing **Iberian Renaissance "ensalada"** — literally "salad," a playful 16th-century genre that stitches sacred and popular tunes into one madcap polyphonic medley (the master of the form was **Mateu Flecha the Elder**). Lineup is four voices plus viola da gamba, vihuela and organ. Earthy, witty, virtuosic early music — the opposite of stiff.

## Wed 1 Jul, 1pm — RCM Strings Showcase: *Light and Shadow*

Lunchtime, conservatoire students, folk-meets-classical theme. Confirmed works: **Bruch**'s String Octet (late-Romantic, warm, generous — Bruch of the famous violin concerto) and **Piazzolla**'s *Histoire du Tango* (Argentine nuevo tango — rhythmic, smoky, very different energy). Strong young players, short and cheap.
