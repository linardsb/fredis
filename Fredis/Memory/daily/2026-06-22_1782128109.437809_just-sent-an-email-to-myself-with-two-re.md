**1) Seared Scallops with Pea Purée & Crispy Pancetta** (serves 6 · ~10 min prep, 15 min cook)

*Ingredients:* 30 king scallops, 240g cubed pancetta, 750g frozen peas, 120g butter, 6 tbsp crème fraîche, 24 mint leaves, 3 garlic cloves (optional), 3 tbsp olive oil, 3 lemons (juice + wedges), flaky salt & pepper.

*Method:*
1. Pat scallops completely dry both sides; remove the tough side-muscle. Dry scallops = best sear.
2. Render pancetta from a cold dry pan over medium heat until deeply golden and crisp. Scoop out; keep the fat.
3. Boil peas in salted water until tender and bright green. Drain, reserving a few tbsp of the water.
4. Blitz peas with half the butter, crème fraîche, mint and optional garlic until smooth, loosening with pea water. Season, keep warm.
5. Heat olive oil in the pan until shimmering. Season dry scallops with salt and sear undisturbed ~1.5 min until a deep crust forms. Work in batches — don't crowd or they steam.
6. Flip, add remaining butter, let it foam and spoon over. Pull them the moment they're just opaque and springy (~1 more min).
7. Swoosh warm purée on each plate, top with scallops and crispy pancetta, finish with lemon and pan butter. Serve with wedges.

*Make-ahead:* purée and pancetta can be done earlier in the day; only the sear is left for serving. With 30 scallops expect 2–3 batches — warm the plates, have everything ready.

---

**2) Summer Berry Pavlova — Gluten & Nut Free** (serves 6)

*Ingredients:* 4 large egg whites (room temp), 200g caster sugar, 1 tsp cornflour, 1 tsp white wine vinegar, 1 tsp vanilla extract, 300ml double cream, 1 tbsp icing sugar, 400g mixed summer berries, mint to garnish.

*Method:*
1. Oven to 150C / 130C fan. Line a tray with parchment; draw a 20cm circle as a guide. Bowl and whisk must be spotlessly grease-free.
2. Whisk egg whites to soft peaks.
3. Add caster sugar a tablespoon at a time, whisking to thick, glossy, stiff peaks — rub a little between fingers to check no grains remain.
4. Sift over the cornflour, add vinegar and vanilla, fold gently (this gives the crisp shell + marshmallow centre).
5. Spoon onto the parchment, building the sides into a shallow nest with a dip in the middle.
6. Bake 1 hour, then turn the oven off and leave inside with the door shut to cool fully — ideally a couple of hours or overnight.
7. Whip cream with icing sugar to soft peaks. Pile into the centre, scatter berries, finish with mint. Serve within the hour.

*Make-ahead:* bake the meringue a day ahead and store airtight. Add cream and berries only in the last hour or it softens.
