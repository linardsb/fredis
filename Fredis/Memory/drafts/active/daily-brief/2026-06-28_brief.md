## 2. From your channels

**[We're frozen out (for good?)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=61dz7Fh0ozg)** — David Shapiro, 2026-06-26. The trigger is government restricting frontier models: OpenAI asked to hold GPT-5.6, Anthropic pushed to pull Mythos and Fable. Shapiro reads this not as a blip but a permanent bifurcation — a cleared flagship for government and top-paying corporate use, a watered-down model for everyone else. He ties it to Aschenbrenner's situational-awareness timeline and years of effective-altruist regulation pressure, arguing tech has always stratified. He pushes a contrarian line that frontier AI is net-positive for security because read-only AI automates pen-testing and auditing. He concedes the outcome is dystopian but historically normal. So what: Frontier pillar — design Cab and Email Hub against a general-tier default, not the flagship you may not be allowed to ship.

**[AI didn't make you faster. It just hid the real bottleneck.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AdtGNAhVtok)** — Nate B Jones, 2026-06-28. Core claim: the meeting to discuss a feature now takes longer than building it, the PRD longer than the prototype, the planning longer than shipping three variants. AI moved the constraint but work habits stayed put. He invokes the manufacturing principle that eliminating a bottleneck relocates it downstream rather than removing it. The implied fix is to cut process ceremony, not build speed. (Transcript was heavily truncated, so this is thin.) So what: lead generation and Email Hub — kill PRD ceremony, ship Email Hub variants and let usage decide.

**[Stop Writing Tone Instructions. Layer Them.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ij-AU9dpJjc)** — AI Engineer (Isadora Martin-Dye), 2026-06-26. A single system prompt works on the happy path but breaks at turn 21 because one prompt can't be situational, expressive and self-checking at once. Her fix is four ordered layers: immutable identity (hard rules), situational mode (user state), example-anchored voice, and a cheap post-generation veto. Order is load-bearing — hard rules first. She collapsed 24 ad-hoc system prompts into one canonical assembly, with AI-disclosure baked in as a layer-one rule. So what: Email Hub — re-architect the agent's voice as layers so brand tone survives edge-case threads instead of one mega-prompt.

**[India Can Create The Largest AI Companies](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ju8LVdvuxGM)** — YC Root Access, 2026-06-27. Ex-YC partners Puneet and Arnav argue this wave rewards living at the edge of the technology over go-to-market or business-model mastery. The mobile wave tokenised labour and created hyper-local network effects; AI is global, so global companies can be built from anywhere for the first time. Puneet built SuperDaily to $100M ARR with effectively one engineer — pre-AI. Their advice: build for the global/US market directly, since AI removes the old need to relocate to SF or find warm intros. The differentiator is understanding the tech 10x better than anyone else. So what: lead generation — position Cab and Email Hub globally from day one and lead with technical depth, not network.
