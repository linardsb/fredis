# UK non-dilutive funding — indicative shortlist (Email Hub + Cab)

**What this is:** where to point limited application effort for non-dilutive money,
scored with the `uk-funding-grants` rubric [V1-C1]. **What this is not:** an
eligibility ruling. Every band below is `confidence: inferred` and conditional on
the entity facts in the next section — the binding "are you eligible / will this be
funded" call is a grant adviser's, not Fredis's (see §Where a human advisor is
needed).

## Read first — the landscape (2026-06-29)

The broad, sector-agnostic UK open grant a founder reaches for first is **not
currently available**: Innovate UK paused Smart Grants in early 2025 and no
sector-agnostic open grant had replaced it on this snapshot date [V1-C2]. What is
actually live and non-dilutive for a solo UK software/AI founder is narrow: **R&D
tax relief** (a tax relief, not a grant) [V1-C3], the **EIC Accelerator grant-only**
route for an EU-ambition project [V1-C4], and **themed/challenge competitions** when
a current theme fits. Everything else is watch-list or out-of-fit. This is why the
shortlists below are short — not an oversight.

## The decisive unknown — your corporate vehicle

The rubric's first hard gate is **entity / registration**, and it decides almost
everything below. The lane registry [V2-C1] does **not** record the facts that close
that gate:

- **Email Hub** — is there a **UK-registered company**? Incorporated when? Any
  employees? Most Innovate UK grants require a UK-registered company [V1-C5]; R&D tax
  relief requires a UK Corporation-Tax-paying company, and the enhanced ERIS route
  needs R&D to be ≥ 30% of total spend [V1-C3].
- **Cab** — Latvia-first, built with Latvian partners [V2-C2], so most likely a
  Latvian/EU entity (or none incorporated yet) — which **fails** the UK-registration
  gate for UK domestic grants.

Until these are confirmed, the gates cannot be closed. **So the tables below are an
indicative ranking of where to look, not a statement of eligibility.**

## Email Hub — indicative shortlist

UK MarTech/SaaS, ~95% built, ship-first, Linards full-time UK [V2-C3]. Scored *as if*
a UK-registered micro-company doing genuine software R&D — an assumption that must be
confirmed (see above).

| Programme | Instrument | Indicative band | The gate that decides it | Next check |
|-----------|-----------|:---------------:|--------------------------|-----------|
| **R&D tax relief** (RDEC / ERIS) | Tax relief (non-dilutive) | **High*** | Needs a UK CT-paying company + qualifying R&D spend [V1-C3] | Book an R&D tax specialist to scope a claim |
| **EIC Accelerator** (grant-only for UK) | Grant (non-dilutive) | **Medium*** | UK SME *and* genuine high-risk innovation with EU-scale ambition; a near-market, mostly-built product may read as too incremental for TRL 6–8 framing [V1-C4]; heavy two-stage process | Sense-check fit with an EIC adviser before committing |
| **Innovate UK themed comps / Contracts for Innovation** | Grant / contract | **Low — watch** | Needs a live competition whose theme matches MarTech; none obvious on the snapshot date [V1-C6] | Check the Innovation Funding Service when a theme lands |
| **Smart Grants** | Grant | **N/A — watch** | Dormant since Jan 2025; no open round [V1-C2] | Watch for a successor reopening |
| **KTP** | Collaborative grant | **Low** | Needs a UK academic/Catapult lead + 2+ employees; poor fit for a solo, near-market product [V1-C7] | Skip unless a university R&D collaboration is wanted anyway |
| **Manchester Prize** | Challenge prize | **Low — watch** | Theme-gated (recent round was net-zero energy); MarTech does not fit known themes [V1-C8] | Watch the Round 3 theme |
| **Tech Nation** | Accelerator / network | **Not funding** | Provides network and pitch-competition exposure, not grant cash [V1-C9] | Optional visibility play, not a funding line |

\* Conditional on confirming the corporate vehicle.

**Short read:** the one realistic near-term *non-dilutive cash* lever is **R&D tax
relief**, if a UK Ltd with genuine R&D exists. **EIC grant-only** is the only sizeable
grant on the board, but it is a heavy lift and borderline on fit. Everything else is
watch-list — do not spend application effort on dormant or closed programmes.

## Cab — indicative shortlist

Cab is a **paused/concept** lane (sequenced behind VTV traction) and most likely a
Latvian/EU entity [V2-C2], so:

- **UK domestic grants** (Innovate UK, Smart Grants, KTP, Manchester Prize) —
  **Ineligible** on the UK-registration gate as the lane is currently shaped [V1-C5],
  [V2-C2].
- **EU routes** (Horizon Europe, EIC) could in principle fit a Latvian SME, but Cab is
  not live and is paused [V2-C4] — **no action now**.
- **R&D tax relief** is a UK scheme; any Latvian R&D incentive is outside this pack's
  scope → a Latvian adviser's call.

**Short read:** no UK non-dilutive action for Cab now. Revisit only if Cab unpauses
with a defined EU entity, and route via an EU/Latvian funding adviser.

## Next actions (concrete)

   This single fact closes most of the gates above.
2. **If a UK Ltd with qualifying R&D spend exists** → book an R&D tax specialist to

3. **Set a watch** (heartbeat / `launch-governance/bet-review`) on: a Smart Grants
   successor reopening, a MarTech-relevant Innovate UK theme, and Manchester Prize
   Round 3 [V1-C2], [V1-C8].
4. **Park grant applications** until a gate-passing programme is actually open.
5. **For any binding call** → a UK grant consultant / R&D tax adviser (Email Hub) or
   an EU/Latvian funding adviser (Cab).
