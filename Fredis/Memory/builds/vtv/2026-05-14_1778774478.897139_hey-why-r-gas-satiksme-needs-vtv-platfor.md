**Fredis:** *Topic: Why VTV (vs patching what RS has)*

Two separate questions hiding inside one — answering both.

**Why Rīgas Satiksme needs the VTV platform**

- RS publicly stated (29 April 2026, LSM) that its electronic payment system is outdated and **several unrelated ticketing systems run in parallel** with limited development potential.
- They've opened a market study *now* — the requirements-shaping window is Q2–Q3 2026, not after the procurement notice.
- The gap is structural: **no unified operations CMS, no AI co-pilot for drivers / dispatchers / planners, no GTFS-RT-clean data layer, no LV-native UI on day one** across any of the foreign incumbents (Optibus / Trapeze / HASTUS / INIT / Cubic).

**Why build from scratch beats "fix what's there" — five reasons**

1. **There's nothing single to fix.** RS's own statement is "*several unrelated systems running in parallel*". "Fixing" means re-integrating N foreign vendors — the integration cost compounds and the IP still leaves Latvia at every renewal.
2. **You can't bolt agentic AI onto a 2010-era stack.** Pydantic-typed tools, async GTFS, sandboxed knowledge ops, swappable on-prem LLM — these require the data model to be right *first*. Wrapping legacy = a wrapper that inherits every legacy constraint.
3. **Sovereignty is architectural, not configurable.** Ollama-on-prem option, source-code escrow, IP-stays-in-Latvia — none of these can be retrofitted into foreign SaaS. They're decided at design time or not at all.
4. **LV-native ≠ translated UI.** Patching adds a Latvian translation layer on top of an English data model. Native-first means RBAC roles, error messages, audit trails, and reporting are Latvian by design — the difference Council will hear in user feedback within 6 months.
5. **Cost curve flips.** Patching = recurring foreign licence + integration spend forever. VTV = milestone-priced build, EU co-financing eligible (CEF Transport 2, RRF digital, Horizon Cluster 5), with RS staff owning it after Phase 3 hand-off.

**The honest reframe — and this is the version that lands with Bartaševičs**

VTV doesn't *replace* the payment system. It sits **alongside** it. RS runs the payment procurement on its own track; VTV delivers the **operations layer that doesn't exist at all today** — unified CMS + AI agent for driver / dispatcher / planner / admin workflows, GTFS-compliant, bilingual, sovereign-deployable. So the "fix vs build" framing is technically wrong: the *payment system* gets fixed (or re-procured) on its own timeline; the *operations + AI layer* is greenfield because nothing comparable exists in the current stack.

That means the political ask isn't "scrap what works and start over". It's "**fund the missing layer**, on a 4-week fixed-price Phase-0, with a kill-switch at every gate". Near-zero downside, evidence on the table inside one month.
