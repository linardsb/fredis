## How to read this document

- Competitors grouped by **country**, then by **tier** (A = ceiling, B = peer, C = local, D = solo).

## United Kingdom

### Tier A — Enterprise boutiques (above my tier, ceiling reference)

**Faculty AI**
- London. UK's most prominent indie AI consultancy pre-acquisition.
- **Key event:** acquired by Accenture January 2026. Operates as a distinct centre of excellence inside Accenture.
- Known for: NHS work during COVID, OpenAI safety partnership.
- **Envy — distribution:** every UK government AI procurement knew their name. That took a decade to build; I'd need a shortcut (content, partnership, or vertical specialisation).

**Mind Foundry**
- Oxford University spin-out. Responsible-AI positioning.
- Clients: insurance, infrastructure, defence (high-stakes sectors).
- Founding team: leading AI researchers. Human-in-the-loop product philosophy.
- **Envy — positioning sharpness:** "Responsible AI for high-stakes sectors" = one crisp sentence. Mine isn't one sentence yet.

**Neurons Lab**
- London + Singapore. Agentic-AI consultancy for mid-to-large BFSIs in regulated environments.
- Messaging: "100% of projects in production or on track for production deployment."
- **Envy — vertical specificity:** BFSI-only in a regulated niche = premium pricing, no scope creep. Vertical always wins on margin.

**Elsewhen**
- London. Agentic systems + generative UI platforms.
- Technical stack: multi-agent orchestration, RAG, live tool integration via MCP.
- Commercial pitch: full IP ownership + vendor independence, production-ready systems vs. "experiments."
- **Envy — the IP-ownership + vendor-independence pitch:** speaks directly to client CTO anxiety about lock-in. A pricing-power argument disguised as a values argument.

**Narwhal Labs**
- UK-based. £20M backed in April 2026 to launch **DeepBlue** — enterprise-grade agentic AI platform.
- **Envy — capital:** £20M war chest = 24 months of enterprise-length sales cycles survivable. I'm bootstrapped.

### Tier C — Sussex / South-East / East-Grinstead local (direct back-yard competitors)

**Darcy Digital**
- Sussex AI consultancy specifically for SMEs.
- Methodology: "3-step process, ROI within 6 months."
- **Envy — productised methodology:** they've turned consulting into a SKU. Custom build can't be priced against a productised method; this is the single clearest competitive move in the Sussex market right now.

**Artemis Marketing**
- East Grinstead. SEO + AI optimisation + digital marketing.
- Multi-award-winning. EOT (employee-owned trust) structure. AI training + consultancy arm.
- **Envy — local content engine + awards:** years of consistent publishing + physical awards on the shelf = they own Sussex SEO for "AI consulting" and have the credentials to back it. I have zero content out yet.

**AI Consultant Sussex** (aiconsultantsussex.co.uk)
- Sussex-wide practical AI for small businesses.
- **Envy — local SEO ranking:** they show up for "AI consultant Sussex" before I do, purely on search-term age.

**Cloudswitched**
- Sussex AI tools for professional services firms (law, accounting, surveyors).
- **Envy — vertical-within-a-region pick:** narrow enough to defend, wide enough to scale through referral.

**Fountain Digital**
- Sussex. Building around **Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO)** — getting businesses to show up in ChatGPT / Gemini / Copilot answers.
- **Envy — the timing bet:** being the Sussex name for AEO in 2026 is a 12-month window they moved on while everyone else was debating whether it matters.

**Blue Frontier**
- Sussex-adjacent. Generative AI consultancy.

## Latvia

**Tilde**

- **Envy — the language-model moat:** nobody else sells Latvian / European-languages LLM work credibly. EC budget flows to them by default. Structural advantage, not hustle.

**Helmes Latvia**
- IT consulting with AI practice. Estonian parent, Latvian operation.
- **Envy — enterprise-integrator relationships:** already inside big Latvian bank + state IT procurement. Slow to build, impossible to shortcut.

**Tet Digital**
- Tet (state telco) digital / AI arm.
- **Envy — captive pipeline:** Tet Group sells to every Latvian enterprise; Tet Digital inherits warm leads. Mine are cold.

**Aure AI**
- Riga. Positioned for "businesses that need clarity before implementation" — strategy + analysis + structured integration.
- **Envy — pre-implementation advisory niche:** "clarity before build" is higher-margin than build itself, and positions them to win the build afterwards. Clean two-step funnel.

**Scandilytics**
- NY + Riga. Generative AI consulting, mid-size team.
- **Envy — cross-border cost-base arbitrage:** NY + Riga means US billing rates on Riga delivery cost base. That's the exact UK↔LV structure my own life plan should be operationalising.

**Accenture Latvia / TietoEvry Latvia**
- Enterprise integrators, above my tier. Ceiling reference.

**MILA — Latvian AI Association**
- Industry body, not competitor. 16 founding member companies (Sept 2024).
- **Position:** join as associate member — the room my competitors are already in.

**Overly** (AR platform, Riga)
- Product-company, not consultancy. Adjacent only.

## Argentina

### Enterprise AI consulting

**Globant**
- Argentine-founded (now multi-HQ). NYSE-listed. ~30k employees.
- Pivoting heavily into AI Native Services. >50% of projects AI-related. AI Pods exit-rate ARR target $60–100M by end-2026.
- **Signature reference:** YPF Digital Suppl.AI (Oct 2025) — agentic AI in production operations.
- Dropped ~70% in 2025 on market re-rate; now viewed as value play.
- **Envy — enterprise-scale proof point:** they've proven the agentic-AI story works at Argentine enterprise scale. Either my ceiling or my reference-case proof, depending on how I position.

**Baufest**
- Argentine-founded, offices across LatAm + US.
- **Envy — regional-boutique footprint:** shows you can scale past solo without selling out to Accenture.

**Faraday**
- Buenos Aires AI / ML development studio. NLP, computer vision, predictive analytics, recommendation systems, from research to production.
- **Envy — pure AI/ML studio positioning:** credentials-first pitch wins in regulated sectors.

**RIM Nexo**
- Buenos Aires AI + automation consultancy for industrial SMEs — manufacturing, oil & gas, construction, logistics.
- **Envy — industrial-SME vertical pick:** unsexy, sticky, high-budget. The margin segment global AI trend-chasers ignore.

**Xcapit / Jampp / Código Byte**
- Buenos Aires AI + custom software shops.
- **Envy — cost-base advantage:** Argentine talent at 60–70% below US rates on equivalent skill. My pitch to AR clients has to be methodology + cross-jurisdiction + EU/UK credibility, never hours.

### Argentine agri-tech (prospective service-line tier — more partners than competitors)

**Bioceres (BIOX)** — NYSE listed
- Agri-biotech. Founded by a group of Argentine farmers. HB4 drought-resistant wheat and soybean traits. Bioinputs, seed tech.
- **Envy — founded-by-farmers credibility:** NYSE-listed with a decades-deep relationship into the actual Argentine ag ecosystem. Authenticity borrowable through partnership, not buildable from outside.

**Auravant**
- Argentine-founded farm-data SaaS. Satellite / UAV imagery, 30 countries, 3.7M hectares under monitoring.
- **Envy — productised geographic spread:** already the aggregation layer for Argentine-built agri-tech internationally.

**Kilimo**
- Argentine-founded. AI + satellite + ML for water optimisation. Raised $7.5M Series A (Emerald Technology Ventures + The Yield Lab Latam + Salkantay Ventures). **72 billion litres of water saved across 7 countries.**
- **Envy — measurable-outcome story:** "72B litres of water saved" is a sellable line. Outcomes-based positioning beats feature-based positioning every time for mid-market farm clients.

**S4 Agtech**
- Argentine. Satellite-imagery-derived productivity index sold to **crop insurers**, not farmers.
- **Envy — insurance-layer niche (B2B2B):** they're not selling to farmers, they're selling to the people who *fund* farmers. Structural leverage.

## What this teaches the brain to flag as opportunities

1. **Productise my methodology into a SKU** (vs. selling custom hours). The Darcy Digital pattern.
2. **Publish consistently** to build local SEO + thought-leadership in Sussex and LV. The Artemis Marketing pattern.
3. **Early timing bets on emerging niches** (AEO, agentic-for-agri, commodity-cycle consulting) before they're crowded. The Fountain Digital / Kilimo pattern.
4. **Fractional / retainer pricing over day-rate projects.** The Nick Masca pattern.
5. **Cross-border UK↔LV structure** that bills UK / EU enterprise rates on Riga delivery cost base. The Scandilytics pattern.
6. **Join MILA (Latvia) and equivalent industry bodies** as a credibility + distribution play while they're still forming.
7. **Cost-base awareness on Argentine engagements** — pitch methodology + cross-jurisdiction + EU/UK credibility, never hours.
8. **Partnership with agri-tech incumbents** (Bioceres-tier) for credibility borrow, rather than trying to build local authenticity from outside.
9. **Outcomes-based positioning** ("X litres saved", "Y% yield uplift") over feature-based positioning ("we do RAG / multi-agent / MCP"). Kilimo showed the way.
10. **B2B2B niche structural-leverage plays** (S4-style — sell to the layer above the end-user: insurers, reinsurers, co-ops, subsidy administrators, grain traders).
