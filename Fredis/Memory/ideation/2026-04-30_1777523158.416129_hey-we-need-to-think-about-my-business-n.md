**Fredis:** *Topic: Saulera colour direction*

Saulera was the right call. It's warm, single-word, story-resilient ("sun-era" works in any service line), and trilingually friendly. Worth checking .lv and .ai on the same root before you build the brand on .com — both make natural geo/tech extensions.

**Framing — what the name asks of the palette**

*Saule* is sun, and sun is the most overused naming motif in tech. The colour scheme either rescues that or doubles down on the cliché. Three things to keep in mind:

- **Don't go literal yellow.** Primary-school-bright sun yellow reads childish, fails contrast on screens, and undercuts advisor positioning.
- **Anchor with a dark.** Tech credibility and longform readability demand a deep tone to balance the warmth. Without it, the brand floats.
- **Bring a third culture.** Saulera is Latvian-rooted; the AR family layer is part of your story. A small terracotta or sky tone earns the bridge without flag-waving.

**Three palette directions**

**A. Baltic-Argentine Sunrise** *(my pick)* — Deep night sky as anchor, Latvian amber as signature, warm cream as ground, terracotta as the Argentine layer. Reads "rooted, calm, sophisticated advisor" — tech-credible without being a SaaS template.

**B. Solar Minimalism** — Near-black + bone + one signal-amber. SaaS/dev-studio adjacent, very modern, low warmth. Strong if you want to compete on a list of AI-tooling brands and look engineer-first.

**C. Latvian Folk-Heritage** — Oxblood + amber + linen + forest green. Rich, woven, deeply on-brand for the LV roots — but risks reading rustic to a CFO. Best as a *future* secondary palette for case studies / content, not the core.

**Direction A — proposed five-token palette**

| Role | Hex | Name | Usage |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Anchor | `#0E1B2C` | Baltic Night | Headers, body text, dark UI |
| Signature | `#D9A441` | Saules Amber | Logo glyph, primary CTAs, highlights |
| Bridge accent | `#B5563E` | Pampa Terracotta | Small accents, callouts (sparingly — 5% rule) |
| Ground | `#F5EFE6` | Linen Cream | Page background, light mode |
| Mid-tone | `#7A8A99` | Iron Mist | Borders, secondary text, muted UI |

This passes WCAG AA on Anchor / Cream and on Anchor / Amber — but Amber on Cream is borderline, so reserve Amber for the glyph and CTA fills, never small body text.

**Pairing notes**

- **Typography.** Pair one humanist sans (Inter, IBM Plex Sans, or Söhne) with a single high-contrast serif for headlines (GT Sectra, Söhne Schmal, or Recoleta). Sans for tech, serif for advisor — the balance Saulera wants.
- **Logo direction.** A *saules krusts* (Latvian sun cross) is heritage-rich but historically loaded — designable but needs care. Cleaner first sketches: a single rising arc, an abstract sun-ray cluster, or a stylised letter Ś / S as the sun glyph. Don't lock palette before the logo direction is sketched — they iterate together.
- **What to ship first.** A one-page brand sheet (palette, two type families, one-line voice statement, logo concept). That alone unblocks the website, LinkedIn header, deck template, and email signature.
