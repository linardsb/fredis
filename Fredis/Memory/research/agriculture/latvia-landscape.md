# Latvia — AI-in-Agriculture Landscape

**Last updated:** 2026-04-16
**Owner:** Linards Berzins
**Purpose:** Evidence base for the Latvian leg of the UK↔LV↔AR AI-in-ag service-line thesis.
**Status:** Working draft — verify details before any commercial action.

---

## Bottom line

The Latvian agri-AI scene is **thin**, and that is the thesis-confirming signal. One named pure-play (AgriMind), one adjacent drone brand, and then a structural gap between the country's strong agri-research infrastructure (LBTU, LLKC, EIT Food LV) and the absence of applied-AI vendors serving mid-market Latvian farms. Latvia's AI specialisation is sales / marketing AI and embedded-ML SaaS — not agriculture. The white space is real, not imagined.

## Institutions — distribution & credibility partners (not competitors)

### LBTU — Latvia University of Life Sciences and Technologies
- **URL:** https://www.lbtu.lv/en
- **Location:** Jelgava
- **Formerly:** Latvia University of Agriculture (LLU), renamed in 2022.
- **Strategy:** 2023–2027 strategy positions LBTU as the **leading science university for bioeconomy innovation in the Baltic Sea region**.
- **Relevant faculty:** Faculty of Agriculture and Food Technology.
- **Role for thesis:** Research partnership, academic credibility, applied-innovation project funding, access to students and graduate pipelines.
- **Entry move:** Audit current live research projects in Faculty of Agriculture & Food Tech. Identify 2–3 with natural applied-AI overlap. Offer industry advisory / guest-lecturer role.

### LLKC — Latvian Rural Advisory and Training Centre (Latvijas Lauku konsultāciju un izglītības centrs)
- **Ownership:** State 99% + Latvian Farmers' Federation 1%.
- **Track record:** 30+ years.
- **Footprint:** **26 regional offices across the entire territory of Latvia.**
- **Services:** State + EU funded advisory on agri-environment, crops, livestock, accounting, economy, and training. Plus commercial advisory services.
- **Role for thesis:** **LLKC is the distribution channel to mid-market Latvian farms.** You cannot replicate 26 regional offices and 30 years of farmer relationships; you partner with them or you lose.
- **Entry move:** Identify the regional office closest to your Latvian network anchor (likely Zemgale / Jelgava given LBTU). Secure an introductory meeting to understand current digital / AI advisory gaps.

### EIT Food — Latvia chapter
- **URL:** https://www.eitfood.eu/in-your-area/latvia
- **Relevant programme:** **Test Farms** — agri-tech startups matched with real European farms for real-world product testing. Runs March–Nov 2026. Max €4k per application.
- **Role for thesis:** Low-risk wedge entry for applied-AI methodology validation on a Latvian pilot farm.
- **Other programme:** EIT Food Accelerator Network (FAN) 2026 — six specialised hubs across Europe for agri-food / food-tech startups.
- **Entry move:** Apply to Test Farms 2026 as a delivery partner, not a product vendor.

### MILA — Latvian Artificial Intelligence Association (Latvijas Mākslīgā intelekta asociācija)
- **URL:** https://www.mila.lv/en
- **Founded:** September 2024.
- **Founding members:** 16 Latvian companies.
- **Role for thesis:** Credibility + distribution bridge for AI-consultancy positioning locally. Access to the rooms other Latvian AI vendors are in.
- **Entry move:** Join as associate member. Volunteer for a working group (particularly any applied-AI or vertical-SaaS group that touches ag / commodities / transport).

### Baltic AI Gigafactory (initiative, 2025)
- **Scope:** Joint Expression of Interest submitted by Poland + Latvia + Lithuania + Estonia to the European Commission (June 2025) for a Baltic AI Gigafactory — large-scale compute infrastructure for regional AI development.
- **Status:** Under EC review; if approved, materially changes the funding / compute environment for applied-AI in the region.
- **Role for thesis:** Track quarterly for funding programme launches that could underwrite a Baltic agri-AI pilot.

## Baltic regional context (Lithuania / Estonia)

Since Latvian pure-play agri-AI is so thin, the realistic "Baltic" service footprint includes Lithuanian and Estonian assets:

- **AUG1L — AUGA Group** (Lithuania, Nasdaq Vilnius) — largest vertically integrated organic agriculture group in Europe. Natural partnership anchor for any Baltic AI-in-ag service line.
- **AKO1L — Akola Group** (Lithuania, Nasdaq Vilnius) — diversified ag holding: grain trading, agri inputs, equipment distribution, poultry, frozen foods. The biggest distribution + data footprint in Baltic ag.
- **PZV1L — Pieno Žvaigždės** and **VLP1L — Vilkyškių Pieninė** (Lithuania) — largest Lithuanian dairy processors. AI in dairy logistics, herd health, milk-quality sensing = relevant adjacent vertical.
- **AgriFood Lithuania DIH** — Lithuanian agri-food digital innovation hub. Cross-border project vehicle.
- **OneSoil** — Belarusian-founded precision-ag SaaS using Copernicus satellite data; operates regionally including Baltics.

## Strategic implications for the service-line thesis

1. **The white space is real.** AgriMind is effectively alone in Latvia. No crowded competition.
2. **LLKC is the distribution lever.** Partner with them or lose — you can't replicate their network.
3. **LBTU is the credibility lever.** Research partnership / advisory role = academic air cover for commercial positioning.
4. **EIT Food Test Farms is the low-risk entry.** A €4k application could secure a Latvian pilot farm to validate methodology.
5. **AgriMind is a partner before a competitor.** They solve local-data problems I'd need to solve anyway; co-sell before compete.
6. **Capital is arriving.** €607M Baltic VC in 2025 (up 20% YoY) + Baltic AI Gigafactory initiative = best funding environment the region has seen.
7. **Don't try to build Latvian farmer trust cold from the UK.** Trust flows through institutions (LLKC, LBTU) and language (Latvian-first conversations), not through outbound marketing.
