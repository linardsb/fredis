# DRAFT — Request for written IP carve-out / confirmation

**Subject:** Clarifying ownership of personal-project work — {{PROJECT_NAME}}

Dear {{RECIPIENT_NAME}},

I am writing to request written clarification regarding the ownership of a personal project I have been working on in my own time, separate from my duties at {{EMPLOYER_NAME}}.

## Summary of the work

- **Project name:** {{PROJECT_NAME}}
- **Brief description:** {{ONE_SENTENCE_DESCRIPTION}}
- **Developed:** on personal hardware, outside contracted working hours, using no {{EMPLOYER_NAME}} confidential information or tools.
- **Relation to my role:** {{HOW_IT_RELATES_OR_DOES_NOT}}

## What I am asking for

I would like written confirmation from {{EMPLOYER_NAME}} that the project as described above:

1. falls **outside** the scope of my normal and specifically-assigned duties as {{JOB_TITLE}}, and therefore outside s.39(1) of the Patents Act 1977; and
2. does **not** comprise "literary, dramatic, musical or artistic work … made in the course of … employment" as defined by s.11(2) of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988; and accordingly
3. {{EMPLOYER_NAME}} asserts no intellectual-property claim in the project or its continued development.

If {{EMPLOYER_NAME}} wishes to assert any claim, I would be grateful if you could identify the specific artefact(s) of concern so that I can address them — whether through a carve-out, a clean-room rebuild, or by withdrawing that element of the project.

## Why I am asking in writing

I want to respect {{EMPLOYER_NAME}}'s rights and my own, and a written position protects both sides from later misunderstanding if the project ever becomes commercialised.

Happy to discuss on a call or in writing — whichever works best for you.

Best regards,

{{YOUR_NAME}}
{{YOUR_JOB_TITLE}}
{{YOUR_CONTACT}}

## Internal notes (delete before sending)

- Tone: factual, cooperative, non-threatening. Avoid legalese unless the employer escalates.
- If the employer asks for more detail, share only the high-level spec — not the implementation.
- If the employer refuses or claims ownership, stop commercial activity and consult a UK IP solicitor before responding.
- Keep a dated PDF of the sent letter + any reply in a personal archive.
