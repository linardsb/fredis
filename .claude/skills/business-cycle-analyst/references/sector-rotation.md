# Sector Rotation Map

The canonical rotation links sector leadership to the short-debt-cycle phase. It is a map, not a clock — sectors don't rotate on a fixed calendar.

## Standard rotation (Sam Stovall / Stock Trader's Almanac)

| Short-cycle phase | Economic regime | Leading sectors | Trailing sectors |
|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Early-cycle** | Recovery, low rates, accelerating growth | Consumer Discretionary, Financials, Industrials, Materials | Utilities, Staples, Healthcare |
| **Mid-cycle** | Stable growth, neutral policy | Technology, Industrials, Communication Services | Utilities, Energy |
| **Late-cycle** | Peak growth, rising rates, inflation risk | Energy, Materials, Consumer Staples | Financials, Discretionary |
| **Recession** | Contracting growth, easing policy | Utilities, Staples, Healthcare, Gold | Discretionary, Financials, Materials |

## Caveats

- The map was built on post-WW2 US data. International markets and the post-2008 regime don't always follow it.
- Technology behaves unusually — structurally growth-heavy, sometimes leads both early and mid-cycle.
- Sector ETFs aggregate differently from the canonical definitions (e.g. Communication Services in GICS includes Meta and Google, skewing the "defensive" interpretation).
- Central-bank regime changes (QE, YCC) distort the rotation by compressing the yield curve's signal.

## How to apply

1. Place the cycle in a phase using the short-debt-cycle framework.
2. Read off the leading-sector set.
3. **Check the "also-factor"** — what structural story is running alongside (energy transition, AI capex, supply-chain reshoring)? These overlay on the rotation and can invalidate it.
4. State the setup in trader voice: "Setup: late-cycle per yield curve + tight labour market. Expected leaders: Energy + Staples. Invalidator: premature recession brings forward Utility outperformance by 6–9 months."

## What this skill will NOT do

- Pick individual equities. Sector calls only.
- Predict timing more precisely than "quarters, not weeks".
- Substitute for fundamentals analysis on specific positions.
