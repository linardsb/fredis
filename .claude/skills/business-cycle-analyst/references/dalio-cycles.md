# Dalio Debt Cycles — Short and Long

Canonical source: Ray Dalio, *How The Economic Machine Works* (video primer); *Principles for Navigating Big Debt Crises* (2018); *Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order* (2021).

## Short-term debt cycle (~5–8 years)

Driven by central-bank credit policy.

| Phase | Signal | Typical duration |
|-------|--------|------------------|
| **Early-cycle** | Rates low, credit expanding, unemployment peaking and falling, sector rotation favours cyclicals | 1–2 years |
| **Mid-cycle** | Growth broad, credit growth steady, profit margins expanding, inflation subdued | 2–3 years |
| **Late-cycle** | Output gap closed, wage growth accelerates, central bank begins tightening, yield curve flattens | 1–2 years |
| **Recession** | Yield curve inverts then un-inverts, unemployment rises, credit spreads widen, policy pivots to easing | 6–18 months |

Key variables to check:
- Yield curve slope (2s10s, 3m10y)
- Unemployment rate direction + level
- Credit spreads (IG and HY)
- Real policy rate vs neutral
- Sector-level EPS revision breadth

## Long-term debt cycle (~50–75 years)

Driven by accumulated debt relative to income. Builds for decades, resolves through deleveraging. Two deleveraging paths:

- **Deflationary** — austerity + defaults. Currency strengthens, asset prices collapse, unemployment rises, social unrest follows.
- **Inflationary** — money-printing + currency debasement. Asset prices nominal-up, real wages fall, currency erodes, capital flight follows.

**"Beautiful deleveraging"** blends both: debt restructuring + modest money printing + fiscal discipline + currency depreciation — such that nominal growth > nominal rates. Rare in practice.

Indicators of long-cycle late-stage:
- Zero-lower-bound policy rates for extended periods
- Central-bank balance sheet > 50% GDP
- Fiscal deficits running through full employment
- Debt service becoming a meaningful share of government revenue
- Reserve-currency status under geopolitical challenge

## How to apply

1. Place the economy in a short-cycle phase using 3–5 of the signals above.
2. Separately, ask whether the long-cycle is constraining the short-cycle (e.g. "the central bank *wants* to tighten but can't afford to").
3. State the phase. State the evidence. State what would invalidate the call.
