# Kondratieff Waves

Canonical sources: Nikolai Kondratieff (1925), Joseph Schumpeter's *Business Cycles* (1939), ongoing debate in heterodox macroeconomics.

## Thesis

Long-wave technological/innovation supercycles of roughly 45–60 years driven by cluster innovations, infrastructure build-out, and eventual saturation. Four "seasons":

| Season | Economy | Financial markets | Typical driver |
|--------|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Spring** | Expansion from post-winter reset; innovation diffusion begins | Equities recover, bonds solid | Infrastructure + new technology |
| **Summer** | Growth accelerates, inflation rises | Commodities lead, bonds weaken | Full industrial build-out |
| **Autumn** | Asset boom, credit expansion, speculative excess | Equities parabolic, bonds rally late | Financialisation of the cycle's winnings |
| **Winter** | Deleveraging, deflationary / disinflationary pressure | Cash + quality, commodities weak | Debt resolution, creative destruction |

## Placement debate

Kondratieff placement is notoriously contested. Multiple credible analysts place the current position differently:
- Some argue **Winter** ended post-GFC and we're in a new Spring driven by AI + energy transition.
- Others argue we're in **late Autumn / early Winter** given debt-to-GDP and demographic headwinds.
- Others argue the framework doesn't apply cleanly since the post-Bretton-Woods monetary regime.

**Rule:** when applying this lens, state which placement you're using and what evidence moves you across the boundary. Never present a K-wave call as consensus.

## Evidence lines you can defend

- **Innovation diffusion** — is the leading technology platform still producing marginal productivity gains, or is it saturating?
- **Credit intensity** — is new credit producing less growth than the prior decade? (Diminishing marginal returns to debt.)
- **Demographics** — is working-age population growing or contracting? (Demographic turns often align with K-wave transitions.)
- **Geopolitical order** — is the reserve-currency / hegemonic arrangement stable or contested?

## How to apply

- Use K-wave for framing *multi-decade* questions (retirement planning, generational career positioning, which industries compound over 20+ years).
- Don't use K-wave for short-cycle calls (1–5 year horizon). The short debt cycle is the right lens there.
- Be honest about uncertainty. If the question can be answered with shorter frameworks, prefer those.

## Caveats

- The K-wave is not empirically clean. Reasonable analysts dispute its existence.
- Even accepting the framework, placement requires a judgement call — disclose it.
